Our question39.9°N · 116.4°E

How do plants build, coordinate and adapt their functional traits?

The Liu Lab examines the links between environment, traits and function. We combine standardized field measurements, global data synthesis, phylogenetic approaches and ecological modelling.

Our work crosses scales—from stomata and leaf nutrients to plant communities and ecosystem productivity—with a particular interest in China's grasslands and the Qinghai–Tibetan Plateau.

Research atlas

Four connected lines of inquiry

A common framework connects each project: environment → trait coordination → ecological function.

01

Trait biogeography

We assemble field observations and global databases to reveal how plant form and function vary across climate, soil and evolutionary gradients.

  • Global synthesis
  • Biogeography
  • Climate gradients
02

Stomatal ecology

We study stomatal size, density, distribution and epidermal packing to connect leaf structure with gas exchange and environmental adaptation.

  • Stomata
  • Leaf anatomy
  • Allometry
03

Biogeochemical niches

We investigate how species organize nitrogen, phosphorus and multiple elements—and what those strategies mean for coexistence and ecosystem function.

  • Nutrients
  • Stoichiometry
  • Assembly
04

Trait networks

We move beyond single traits to ask how trait coordination and higher-order structure respond to global change and regulate productivity.

  • Networks
  • Global change
  • Productivity

Congcong Liu · 刘聪聪

Associate Professor · Plant Functional Ecologist

Connecting rigorous data with consequential ecological questions.

Congcong Liu leads the Plant Microstructure and Functional Ecology Lab in the College of Life and Environmental Sciences at Minzu University of China. His research focuses on the mechanisms linking environmental gradients, plant functional traits and ecosystem processes.
